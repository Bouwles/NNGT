How to Read TABS

Guitar TABS (short for tablature) are a simplified way to notate music for stringed instruments. They show you where to place your fingers on the fretboard rather than requiring you to read standard musical notation.

  • Strings: Each line of a TAB represents one of the guitar strings, with the top line being the high E (thinnest string) and the bottom line being the low E (thickest string).
  • Numbers: Numbers on the lines indicate which fret to press. For example, "3" on the top line means press the 3rd fret on the high E string.
  • Chords: Numbers stacked vertically mean you play all those notes at the same time, forming a chord.
  • String Position:The string positions go from bottom to top, where the bottom has the thickest strings and the higher it goes the thinner, EADGBE.
  • Symbols: Other symbols in TABS indicate techniques:
    • h: Hammer-on
    • p: Pull-off
    • /: Slide up
    • \: Slide down
    • b: Bend
    • ~: Vibrato

Example:

e|-----------------------|
B|-----------------------|
G|-----------------------|
D|-----------------------|
A|-----------------------|
E|--0--3--5--0--3--3--5--|

This was just a basic 0-3-5 riff.
